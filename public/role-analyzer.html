Risks & Strategic Recommendations

The analysis identified three critical risks to the organization's stability and proposes a four-step plan to build a more resilient and sustainable future for the KAA.

Critical Risk Factors

There is no publicly accessible list of a Board of Directors. This creates a significant gap in transparency, accountability, and the necessary support structure. It is unclear to whom the President is accountable and how fiduciary duties are being met.

Public records show conflicting information, listing the KAA as both "Kokomo Art Association Inc." (federal) and "Kokomo Art Association, LLC" (local). This legal ambiguity has serious implications for liability, governance, and compliance.

The organization's stability is highly dependent on the President, who is involved in nearly every facet of activity. This concentration of responsibility creates a substantial risk to organizational continuity and sustainability should the leader step down.

A 4-Step Resilience Plan

1

Resolve Foundations

Immediately engage legal counsel to resolve the Inc/LLC discrepancy and formally constitute, document, and publicize a Board of Directors with clear bylaws.

2

Refine Role Description

Rewrite the Presidential role description to accurately reflect the "Working President" model, replacing vague corporate language with precise, blended responsibilities.

3

Delegate to Empower

Create and empower a supporting leadership structure (e.g., Treasurer, Secretary, Committees) to formally delegate operational and managerial tasks.

4

Develop Succession Plan

Actively identify and cultivate future leaders from within the volunteer pool to ensure long-term continuity and build a culture of shared leadership.
